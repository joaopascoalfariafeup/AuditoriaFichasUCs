## Semestre

## Objetivos
<p><em>O estudante deve conhecer e identificar as principais patologias do trabalho, bem como os agentes toxicológicos mais significativos e respetivas consequências.</em></p>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p><em>Os conteúdos programáticos procuram abarcar todo o espetro das patologias associadas ao trabalho, bem como os agentes toxicológicos e respetivas consequências por forma a que o estudante, no final, esteja apto a reconhecer e fazer o respetivo diagnostico.<br /></em></p>
<p>A aprendizagem é desenvolvida em duas fases fundamenais. Uma primeira na qual são apresentados os conceitos teóricos e as envolventes das diferentes patologias e uma segunda de consolidação de conhecimentos em que a discussão de casos práticos vai permitir a consolidação dos conhecimentos anteriormente adquiridos.</p>
<p>Esta UC será lecionada em módulos temáticos de cariz teórico para enquadramento  geral das áreas científicas nele previstas, associadas à discussão de casos práticos.</p>
<p>Pretende-se, deste modo, expor os conhecimentos mais recentes sobre a etiopatogenia, fisiopatologia, clínica, prognóstico e orientação terapêutica e médico-legal das principais doenças do foro ocupacional, subsequentemente consolidados pela apresentação e discussão de casos clínicos em contexto profissional, considerando os objetivos pedagógicos de aquisição, em complementaridade, dos conhecimentos técnico-científicos e das competências básicas para a interpretação de dados clínicos objetiváveis e das exposições profissionais de risco para a saúde do trabalhador.</p>
<p><em> </em></p>

# Ficha a avaliar - versão em inglês

## Unidade curricular
Occupational Pathology and Toxicology

## Curso responsável
Master in Occupational Safety and Hygiene Engineering

## Créditos ECTS
6

## Ano
2

## Semestre
1

## Objetivos
<p><em>The student must know and identify the main pathologies of the work, as well as the most significant toxicological agents and their consequences.</em></p>

## Resultados de aprendizagem e competências
<p>The program contents seek to cover the whole spectrum of pathologies associated with work, as well as toxicological agents and their consequences so that the student, at the end, is able to recognize and make the respective diagnosis.</p>
<p>Learning is developed in two fundamental stages. A first one in which the theoretical concepts and the surroundings of the different pathologies are presented and a second one of knowledge consolidation in which the discussion of practical cases will allow the consolidation of the knowledge previously acquired.</p>
<p>This course will be taught in thematic modules of theoretical nature to the general framing of the scientific areas provided for it, associated to the discussion of practical cases.</p>
<p>It is intended, therefore, to present the most recent knowledge on the pathophysiology, pathophysiology, clinical, prognosis and therapeutic and medical-legal orientation of the main occupational diseases. Subsequently, they are consolidated by the presentation and discussion of clinical cases in a professional context, considering the pedagogical objectives of acquiring, in complementarity, the technical-scientific knowledge and the basic competences for the interpretation of objectifiable clinical data and professional exposures of health risk of the worker.</p>
<p><br /><br /></p>

## Programa
<p>PATHOLOGY</p>
<ol>
<li>Historical evolution of scientific and clinical research in Occupational Pathology.</li>
<li>Occupational and environmental etiopathogenic mechanisms.</li>
<li>Surveillance and protection of worker's health: general principles and professional ethics.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with non-ionizing electromagnetic radiation.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with vibrations (hand-arm and whole-body vibratory disease), sound vibrations (vibro-acoustic disease) and baropathology.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with risk factors and physical hazards: work-related musculoskeletal injuries: upper and lower limbs</li>
<li>Pathology associated with risk factors and physical hazards: work-related musculoskeletal injuries: spine</li>
<li>Pathology associated with biological risk factors: occupational agents of animal origin.</li>
<li>Occupational immuno-allergic and toxic diseases of animal origin.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with biological risk factors: exposure to occupational agents of vegetal and fungal origin.</li>
<li>Immune-allergic and toxic occupational diseases of vegetable and fungal origin</li>
<li>Pathology associated with ionizing radiation.</li>
<li>Radiation monitoring and protection.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with metals and metal compounds.</li>
<li>Occupational diseases associated with exposure to aluminum, arsenic, beryllium, cobalt, cadmium, chromium, nickel, manganese, lead and mercury.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with organic solvents.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with metallic and metallo-mechanical fluids and fumes.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with oils, hydrocarbons and other petroleum derivatives.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with nitrogenous and fluorinated organic compounds.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with chemical agents of plastics, synthetic textiles and rubber.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with toxic-irritating gases, vapors and fumes.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with inorganic dusts.</li>
<li>Pathology associated with textile fibers and dusts.</li>
<li>Injuries, injuries and functional damage due to occupational accidents.</li>
<li>Anatomic location, type of injury, severity and repair.</li>
<li>Maternal and child health and genetic heritage.</li>
